About hatha yoga

What is hatha

Hatha yoga is a branch of yoga that focuses on physical postures (asanas) and breathing exercises (pranayama) to enhance physical and mental well-being. It is a holistic practice that emphasizes the harmonization of body, mind, and spirit. The word "hatha" comes from two Sanskrit words: "ha," meaning "sun," and "tha" meaning "moon." This refers to the balance of opposites, light, and darkness, masculine and feminine, which are believed to exist within each individual.

why hatha

Hatha yoga includes a wide range of asanas, from gentle stretches to challenging balancing poses, and focuses on aligning the body and breath to promote relaxation, flexibility, strength, and balance. In addition to asanas, Hatha yoga also incorporates pranayama (breathing techniques) and meditation to enhance relaxation and reduce stress. Hatha yoga is often practiced as a physical exercise, but it also has spiritual and philosophical components that aim to help individuals achieve a state of balance, clarity, and inner peace.
